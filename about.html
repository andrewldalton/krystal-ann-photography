My Story

From the sidelines to the studio

01

Hi, I'm Krystal — an Omaha-based photographer, mom, and lover of golden hour light and candid belly laughs.

“Something clicked. Not just the shutter. Something in me.”

Photography has always been something I loved, but life has a funny way of pulling you away from the things that matter. For a while, my camera sat on a shelf collecting dust. Then one spring evening, I was on the sidelines watching youth softball — my kid running the bases, the setting sun hitting just right, parents screaming their hearts out — and something clicked. Not just the shutter. Something in me.

02

I picked up my camera and started shooting. Families in my neighborhood, friends at the park, relatives at holiday gatherings. Every session reminded me why I fell in love with this in the first place: it's not about getting a perfect pose. It's about freezing a moment that would otherwise disappear forever.

“The best photos happen when people forget there’s a camera.”

03

Today, I specialize in family, sports, couples and engagement, and children's milestone photography throughout Omaha and the surrounding communities. My style is relaxed, natural, and rooted in genuine connection.

I have a soft spot for nervous clients, energetic toddlers, and dogs that refuse to sit still. I'm patient, flexible, and I will absolutely get down in the grass to get the right shot.

My Approach

Why Omaha Families Choose Krystal Ann

I don't believe in stiff poses, forced smiles, or sessions that feel like a chore. Here's what you can expect.

Zero pressure

I guide you gently. No barking directions, no “look more natural” — I just create the conditions for great photos.

Kid-friendly

I've photographed enough toddlers to know that bribery sometimes works. We take breaks, we play, and we get great shots.

Location scouting

Not sure where to shoot? I know Omaha's best outdoor spots — from Elmwood Park to Fontenelle Forest and beyond.

Care in every edit

Each photo is individually edited with warmth and intention. I don't mass-export — I make sure every image feels right.
